# Projectile motion

<Row>
<Column>

## Horizontal motion

$$
\begin{align*}
v_x &= v_{0x} = v_0 \cos\theta \\
x &= (v_0 \cos\theta) t
\end{align*}
$$

</Column>
<Column>

## Vertical motion

$$
\begin{align*}
v_y &= v_0 \sin\theta - gt \\
y &= (v_0 \sin\theta)t - \frac{1}{2}gt^2
\end{align*}
$$

</Column>
</Row>

## Parabolic trajectory of the projectile

$$
y = x\tan\theta - x^2\frac{g}{2v_0^2\cos^2\theta}
$$

<Row>
<Column>

Time to reach max height:
$$
T = \frac{v_0\sin\theta}{g}
$$

Total air time:
$$
t = 2T
$$

</Column>
<Column>

Maximum height:
$$
H = \frac{v_0^2\sin^2\theta}{2g}
$$

Horizontal distance:
$$
D = v_xt = \frac{2v_0^2\sin\theta\cos\theta}{g}
$$

</Column>
</Row>

## Center of mass

<Row>
<Column size="7">

$$
\begin{align*}
\vec r_{cm} &= \frac{\sum\limits_i m_i \vec r_i}{M} \\
\vec r_{cm} &= x_{cm} \hat i + y_{cm} \hat j + z_{cm} \hat k \\
\end{align*}
$$

- $r_{cm}$: position vector of center of mass
- $M$: total mass of all point masses

:::info[Center of gravity]

If $\vec g$ is the same at all points on a body, CM = CG and $\vec a_{cm} = \vec g$

:::
