# Temperature and heat

## Units of temperature

<Row>
<Column size="4">

$$
\begin{align*}
T_F &= \frac{9}{5}T_C + 32 \\
T_K &= T_C + 273.15
\end{align*}
$$

</Column>
<Column size="8">

:::info

- **Triple point of water**: solid ice, liquid water and water vapour coexist at 273.16 K (= 0.01 &deg;C) and a vapour pressure of 612 Pa (about 0.006 atm)
- **Absolute zero**: 0 K (= -273.15 &deg;C)

:::

</Column>
</Row>

## Thermal expansion

<Row>
<Column>

### Linear expansion

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta L = L_0\alpha\Delta T
\end{align*}
$$

- $\alpha$: coefficient of linear expansion (K$^{-1}$)
- When an object under goes thermal expansion, any holes in the object expand as well.

</Column>
<Column>

### Volume expansion

$$
\begin{align*}
\Delta V
&= V_0\beta\Delta T \\
&= 3V_0\alpha\Delta T
\end{align*}
$$

- $\beta$: coefficient of volume expansion (K$^{-1}$)
  - $\beta = 3\alpha$

</Column>
</Row>

## Heat

<Row>
<Column>

### Heat capacity

$$
\begin{align*}
Q
&= mc\Delta T \\
&= nC\Delta T \\
\end{align*}
$$

- $c$ : specific heat capacity (J/kg $\cdot$ K)
- $C = Mc$ : molar heat capacity (J/mol $\cdot$ K)
  - $m = nM$

</Column>
<Column>

### Latent heat

$$
\begin{align*}
Q = mL
\end{align*}
$$

- $L_f$: latent heat of fusion (J/kg)
- $L_v$: latent heat of vaporization (J/kg)

</Column>
</Row>

## Heat transfer

<Row>
<Column>

### Conduction

Through physical contact from hot to cold

$$
\begin{align*}
H &= kA\frac{T_H-T_C}{L} \\
H &= -kA\frac{dT}{dx} = \frac{dQ}{dT}
\end{align*}
$$

- $k$: thermal conductivity (W/m $\cdot$ K)
- $A$: cross-sectional area
- $L$: length of the conductor
- Thermal resistance $R = \frac{L}{k}$ (m$^2$/W $\cdot$ K)

:::note[Example]

To find the temperature of the junction between two different conductors:

$$
\begin{align*}
k_1A_1\frac{T_H-T}{L_1} = k_2A_2\frac{T-T_C}{L_2}
\end{align*}
$$

:::

</Column>
<Column>

### Radiation

Through electromagnetic wave which does not require a medium

**Stephan-Boltzmann law**:

$$
\begin{align*}
H_{rad} &= Ae\sigma T^4 \\
H_{net} &= Ae\sigma(T^4-T_S^4)
\end{align*}
$$

- *sigma* $\sigma = 5.67\times10^{-8}$ W/m$^2\cdot$ K$^4$ : **Stephan-Boltzmann constant**
- $e$: emissivity ($0 \le e \le 1$)
- $A$: total surface area
- $T_S$: temperature of surroundings

</Column>
</Row>
